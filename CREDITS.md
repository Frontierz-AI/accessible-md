# Credits

## About this project

accessible-md exists because accessibility knowledge should not be locked away in scattered support pages and tribal knowledge. AI systems are becoming a primary interface for many people with disabilities, and those systems need structured, accurate references to provide real help.

This project was made possible through collaboration between **Frontierz** and the **Microsoft Accessibility team**.

## Frontierz

[Frontierz](https://frontierz.com) builds AI-powered tools that make technology more accessible. The team researched, wrote, verified, and structured every reference in this repository, drawing on hands-on testing with assistive technology, official documentation, community resources, and real conversations with people who are blind or have low vision.

## Microsoft Accessibility

The Microsoft Accessibility team provided guidance, domain expertise, and support throughout the development of the first reference (Microsoft Outlook for Windows). Their ongoing work to improve the accessibility of Microsoft products is what makes references like this one possible in the first place.

Special thanks to:

- **Jeremy Curry** — for his leadership and support of the accessibility initiative that led to this project
- **Anne Taylor** — for her expertise in accessible design and assistive technology workflows
- **Sharon Ewing** — for her deep knowledge of screen reader interaction patterns and real-world accessibility challenges
